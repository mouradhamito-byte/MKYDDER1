Trip Tips & Good to Know

Helpful reminders for smoother travel, driving, and local interactions.

  • Arriving by ferry: after customs, collect all your car documents, especially the green vehicle registration paper.
  • Exchange only around €200. You will likely spend about €100 on car insurance and around €20 on a SIM card, plus small expenses.
  • Mobile SIM option: Vacay WiFi (€19: 4G for 14 days with 10 minutes of national calls): inwi Vacay WiFi.
  • When parking, if someone asks for payment, request a ticket. If they cannot provide one, around 5 DH (about €0.50) is reasonable.
  • Drive carefully and expect motorbikes to move unpredictably and take chaotic routes (LOL).
  • Carry printed copies of your accommodation details (for example Tangier or Chefchaouen).
  • In Morocco, Police Nationale and Gendarmerie Nationale are separate agencies: police mostly in urban zones, gendarmerie in rural and peri-urban zones.
  • On highways you will mostly encounter gendarmerie; inside cities, police are in charge. Both are generally friendly and helpful if you follow rules and speed limits.
  • Always ask permission before taking photos of people, especially in souks.
  • Magic Word: A simple "Salam Alaykoum" is a great way to greet people and start conversations.
  • Download this page for offline access, and consider saving maps on your phone as well. With offline maps, you can still use GPS and navigate even without an internet connection. One of the best apps to use in Morocco is Waze.

Suggested stops by city with quick notes.

Tangier - Kasbah & Medina

Historic lanes, sea views, and a great first stop for a relaxed city walk.

Chefchaouen - Blue Streets

Iconic blue alleys, small craft shops, and scenic mountain-town atmosphere.

Fes - Old Medina

A dense historical center with artisan markets and traditional architecture.

Mohammedia - Corniche

Seafront promenade ideal for evening walks and relaxed family time.

Bouznika - Beachfront

Open sandy shoreline that works well for a calm day trip by the ocean.

Marrakech - Jemaa el-Fna

Lively central square with cultural performances, food stalls, and energy.

Rabat - Kasbah of the Udayas

Fortified district with ocean-facing viewpoints and scenic streets.

Casablanca - Hassan II Mosque Area

One of Morocco’s most famous landmarks with dramatic oceanfront views.

Famous Foods To Try By City

Food ideas with photos and short descriptions for each stop.

Tangier - Fish Tagine

Fresh coastal fish slow-cooked with spices, vegetables, and preserved lemon.

Chefchaouen - Goat Cheese Plates

Local goat cheese often served with olive oil, bread, and mountain herbs.

Fes - Pastilla

Classic sweet-savory pastry with crisp layers, spiced filling, and cinnamon sugar.

Mohammedia - Grilled Seafood

Simple charcoal-grilled fish and shellfish, popular around the coastline.

Bouznika - Friday Couscous

Steamed semolina with vegetables and meat, a family-style Moroccan staple.

Marrakech - Tanjia

Marrakech specialty of slow-cooked meat prepared in an earthen pot.

Rabat - Harira

Comforting tomato-lentil soup with herbs, often enjoyed with dates and bread.

Casablanca - Briouats

Crispy stuffed pastries, savory or sweet, perfect for sharing snacks.
